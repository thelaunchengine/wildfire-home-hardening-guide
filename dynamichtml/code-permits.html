Building Regulations

If you are planning to make a repair, alteration, or retrofit to your home, you must ensure it follows all applicable building regulations adopted by your jurisdiction. This may require you to consult both your jurisdiction’s local building regulations AND the California Building Code and/or California Residential Code to understand the full set of building requirements that may apply to your project. Building and Construction Requirements for Wildfire

Special building and construction requirements may apply if you are undertaking a building or construction project (including retrofits, repairs, or alterations) in a very high fire hazard severity zone, high fire hazard area, or wildland-urban interface (WUI), as designated by the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection and/or your local jurisdiction.

The California Building Code and the California Residential Code have requirements for building in designated wildland-urban interface fire areas. These requirements are adopted in:

  • California Building Code Chapter 7A – Materials and Construction Methods for Exterior Wildfire Exposure (2022)
  • California Residential Code Chapter 3, Section R337 Materials and Construction Methods for Exterior Wildfire Exposure (2022)

Local Requirements for Wildfire

Many jurisdictions have made local amendments to the State’s building code and/or have adopted additional standards for building and construction in wildfire-prone areas. NOTE! It may be necessary to consult both state and local codes to understand the full set of building design and construction requirements for wildfire.

Santa Barbara County (including unincorporated towns and communities):

County residents in the High Fire Hazard Area must meet special construction requirements for wildfire. Consult the County’s Amendments to the Primary Building Code (Section 10-2.1.2 – Amendments) and Primary Residential Building Code (Sec. 10-2.2.2. – Amendments) for specific information on locally adopted amendments to California Building Code Chapter 7A and Residential Code R337. The County provides a High Fire Notes and Details Sheet for additional guidance.

Town of Montecito:

In addition to County building and construction requirements, residents in Montecito are also subject to the Montecito Fire Protection District Development Standards. This includes Roofing Assembly Requirements for any applicable new or replacement roofing assembly project.

City of Carpinteria:

(TBA, requires future input/confirmation from local jurisdiction)

City of Goleta:

Amendments for construction in High Fire Hazard Areas are provided in Section 15.01.020 Amendments in the City of Goleta Municipal Code. Amendments include a requirement that any roof covering applied in the alteration, repair, or replacement of the roof of any existing structure shall be a fire-retardant roof covering that is at least Class A throughout the City of Goleta.

City of Santa Barbara:

Residents in the City of Santa Barbara that are in a High Fire Hazard Area, as defined by the City of Santa Barbara Fire Department, must meet requirements in the Santa Barbara Municipal Code. Consult the City’s Amendments to the Building Code (Section 22.04.020) and Amendments to the California Residential Code (Section 22.04.025) for specific information on locally adopted amendments to California Building Code Chapter 701A and Residential Code R337. The City of Santa Barbara also provides High Fire Requirements guidance.

Local Requirements for Wildfire

Many jurisdictions have made local amendments to the State’s building code and/or have adopted additional standards for building and construction in wildfire-prone areas. NOTE! It may be necessary to consult both state and local codes to understand the full set of building design and construction requirements for wildfire.

Santa Barbara County (including unincorporated towns and communities):

Building permit exemptions for the County of Santa Barbara include, but are not limited to:

  • One-story detached accessory buildings used as tool and storage sheds, patio covers, playhouses, playground or athletic equipment and similar uses provided the floor area does not exceed 120 square feet and does not exceed 12 feet in height.
  • Wood fences not over 8 feet high and fences of other materials not over 6 feet high, measured from the lowest adjacent grade to the top of the fence on the side which yields the greatest height, unless supporting a surcharge or structural element.
  • Platforms, walks and wood decks not more than 30 inches above grade at any point and not over any basement or story below, nor supporting any structure above, and not part of an accessible route.
  • Roofing repairs less than 100 square feet within a twelve month period.

See Sec. 10-1.6. – Section 105.2 of the California Building Code—Replaced (Section 105.2 Work exempt from permit) for additional information and contact your local building department to confirm your work is exempt before proceeding.

City of Santa Barbara

Building permit exemptions for the City of Santa Barbara include, but are not limited to:

  • One-story detached residential accessory structures used as tool and storage sheds, playhouses, portable and fixed playground equipment, bicycle or skateboard ramps and similar uses, provided the floor area does not exceed 120 square feet (11 m2) and the height does not exceed 10 feet at the highest point; and further provided the structure does not encroach into required setbacks or required open yards, does not obstruct required parking, and is not served by any utilities. The combined square footage of exempt accessory structures may not exceed 200 square feet on any single parcel.
  • Residential fences and walls not over 3½ feet high, as measured from the lowest adjacent grade within 5 feet of the fence or wall that do not adversely affect drainage or cause erosion.
  • Uncovered residential platforms, decks, porches, walks, and similar structures not more than 10 inches above adjacent grade, not over any basement or story below, not part of an accessible route, and not part of the means of egress from a normally occupied space.
  • Roofing repairs less than 100 square feet within a twelve month period.

See the following sections for additional information: 22.04.020 Amendments to the California Building Code (Section 105.2 Work Exempt from Permit) and 22.04.025 Amendments to the California Residential Code (Section R105.2 Work Exempt from Permit), and contact your local building department to confirm your work is exempt before proceeding.

City of Carpinteria:

You can review the City of Carpinteria’s brochure on When Permits are Required to help guide whether your project is exempt from a building permit. Contact your local building department to confirm your work is exempt before proceeding.
