Siding (what the external walls of your home are covered with) can either protect your home or make your home more exposed to fire. If your home has combustible siding, it can ignite or melt and become a conduit for flames to enter vulnerable parts of your home, such as windows or the under-eave area, and stud cavities (then potentially entering into your attic). If siding is ignited, it can move flames and/or embers to the horizontal or vertical lap joints of your home and allow flames to enter.

Combustible siding can also be ignited by other building components, such as decks or fences, vegetation, or radiant heat from another structure (within 30 feet of your home). Thus, it is best to have noncombustible siding on your home, such as stone, stucco, metal (no aluminum), concrete, or brick. However, if that isn’t feasible, there are several things you can do to protect your home if you have combustible siding.

While this major upgrade to your home is costly, it is a long-term investment to protect your home against wildfire. noncombustible siding on your home, such as stone, stucco, metal, concrete, or brick are optimal choices. In particular, if you have older wooden siding, this is an important consideration.

If you currently have combustible siding, such as vinyl, wood or composite materials, incorporating a noncombustible or low-combustible zone within the 0-5 foot zone next to your home will reduce the vulnerability of your siding and home to ignition. Paying careful attention to the area immediately adjacent to your home will help to protect your siding.

Regardless of the type of siding you have, considering noncombustible landscaping and a clear zone immediately adjacent to the home is important. These actions will increase your home’s resilience to ignition from a fire.

If you choose not to replace combustible siding, the use of metal flashing and fire-retardant gypsum board at its base can help to reduce ignition potential. Six inches or more of vertical noncombustible material should be maintained between the ground and the start of the siding.

What is the Wildfire Preparedness Evaluation?

A Wildfire Preparedness Evaluation is a free and voluntary review of the vulnerability of your house and landscape to wildfire and ember-ignition. During this assessment, an evaluator will:

  • review defensible space and home hardening principles
  • make specific recommendations based on your property
  • review your evacuation preparedness so you can be safer and feel more prepared

For your convenience, we have provided three ways for you to receive an evaluation:

  • Complete the evaluation entirely by yourself
  • Request an in-person expert to evaluate your property
  • Hybrid option – Complete as much of the evaluation as possible by yourself and a SBFSC staff member can finish the report in person
