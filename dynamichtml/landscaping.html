Zone 0, the zone within 5 feet of your home, should be the most important area to focus on when it comes to making changes in the immediate vicinity of your home to reduce your home’s vulnerability to embers and ignition. Creating an ember-resistant zone around the home means that nothing combustible is placed in this area, such as mulch, stored wood, combustible fencing or furniture, and any other items that are flammable, such as combustible liquids like propane, or flammable doormats. The selection, location and maintenance of surrounding vegetation and landscaping is also an important factor in protecting your home from ember ignition see https://defensiblespace.org/plants/.

This area is an excellent place to consider walkways constructed out of gravel, concrete, bricks, or other noncombustible materials, or similar hardscape. Landscaping in this area should only consist of non-flammable, non “woody” vegetation types. Considering exterior construction materials, such as siding, vents, roofs, gutters, and windows are all important parts of Zone 0 protection.

If any combustibles are located in this area, remove them and place them at least 5 feet away from your home. Items such as mulch, woody vegetation stored firewood, combustible fencing or furniture, and any other items that are flammable, such as combustible liquids like propane, should be stored or moved at least 5 feet away from the edge of your home.

What is the Wildfire Preparedness Evaluation?

A Wildfire Preparedness Evaluation is a free and voluntary review of the vulnerability of your house and landscape to wildfire and ember-ignition. During this assessment, an evaluator will:

  • review defensible space and home hardening principles
  • make specific recommendations based on your property
  • review your evacuation preparedness so you can be safer and feel more prepared

For your convenience, we have provided three ways for you to receive an evaluation:

  • Complete the evaluation entirely by yourself
  • Request an in-person expert to evaluate your property
  • Hybrid option – Complete as much of the evaluation as possible by yourself and a SBFSC staff member can finish the report in person
