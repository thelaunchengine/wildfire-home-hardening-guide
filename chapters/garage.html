How to best protect your garage by making sure it is noncombustible and sealed with items properly stored to help prevent the ignition of its contents and your home.

Recent wildfires have shown garage doors to be a potential entry point for embers to ignite the structure, increasing the risk of home ignition even if the garage is detached. Make sure your garage is constructed of noncombustible materials and sealed with newer weatherstripping, and fill any cracks in the framing. Inventory the garage contents to make sure any combustible items are properly stored and kept tidy. Finally, know how to manually operate your garage door in the event of a power outage, install a functioning battery backup system, and prepare properly for evacuation.

Make sure that the weatherstripping around your garage (where the edges of your garage meet the sidewalls and ground) is not cracked or damaged and is still flush with the garage and its sides. Two signs your weatherstripping needs to be replaced: if you can slide anything larger than a credit card under the weatherstripping and if you can see any light shining through the seal while inside the garage with the doors closed. Replace worn weather stripping as these are areas where embers could find their way in and ignite the items inside the garage. The same requirement applies to any side entry doors into the garage.

Placing metal guards along the garage trim creates an additional barrier against embers entering the garage. They should be used in addition to secure weatherstripping.

If you have a wooden garage door, particularly if it does not have a solid. If there are cracks or gaps in the siding or framing, they should be filled with caulking or fireproof sealant. Inspecting garage door windows from both the inside and outside is an important step to make sure they aren't separated from the door and have gaps where embers can enter. A simple fix for this is to epoxy the openings to ensure they are sealed.

Maintain fire and ember-resistant trim by sealing any openings or cracks on the garage door, garage siding, and entry doors into the garage. Seal any gaps with a sealant like caulking.

Store all combustible and flammable liquids away from ignition sources. Propane tanks should not be stored in the garage. It's best to keep flammable materials in a metal storage container or cabinet in the back of the garage, away from potential ember intrusions. Finally, keep your garage organized and clutter-free.
